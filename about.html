About Us

Welcome to Healthy Habits! Our mission is to help you make informed decisions about your health with easy-to-use calculators for managing your nutrition and fitness goals. Our Calorie Calculator uses the Revised Harris-Benedict Equation to estimate your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE), considering your activity level to determine your daily calorie needs. Whether you're aiming for weight loss, gain, or maintenance, our tool is designed to support your goals. Additionally, our BMI Calculator provides a quick check on whether your weight is within a healthy range based on your height.

Formulas We Use

Revised Harris-Benedict Equation:

To calculate your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) using the Revised Harris-Benedict Equation:

For Men: \[ BMR = 88.362 + (13.397 \times \text{weight in kg}) + (4.799 \times \text{height in cm}) - (5.677 \times \text{age in years}) \]

For Women:

\[ BMR = 447.593 + (9.247 \times \text{weight in kg}) + (3.098 \times \text{height in cm}) - (4.330 \times \text{age in years}) \]

After calculating your BMR, we multiply it by an activity factor (ranging from sedentary to very active) to estimate your TDEE(Total Daily Energy Expenditure).

BMI Calculation: The Body Mass Index (BMI) is a quick and simple method to assess whether you are in a healthy weight range for your height.

The Body Mass Index (BMI) is calculated using the following formula:

\[ \text{BMI} = \frac{\text{Weight (kg)}}{\text{Height (m)}^2} \]

Based on your BMI, you will be categorized into one of the following ranges: Underweight, Normal weight, Overweight, or Obese.
